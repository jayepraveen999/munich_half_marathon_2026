🏁 Race Day Strategy
  • Target: Sub 2:30 (safe) / Sub 2:15 (stretch).
  • Split strategy: Even or slight negative split — first 10k conservative, second 11k push if feeling good.
  • First 3k: Resist the crowd, settle into rhythm. Run by feel not GPS.
  • Km 15–18: This is where the race starts. Stay focused, maintain form.
  • Fuel: One gel at km 8–10 if practiced in training.
  • Warm-up: 10 min easy jog + drills, 30 min before gun.
⚡ Key Session Types
  • Strides (Base, W1–2): 4×100m relaxed fast running after an easy run — builds neuromuscular speed without fatigue.
  • 400m intervals (Base, W3): 4×400m at 5:30–5:50/km with 2 min rest. Short, sharp, develops efficiency.
  • Tempo (Build): 3–5k continuous at 5:50–6:20/km — comfortably hard.
  • 1k intervals (Build): 4×1k at 5:30–5:50/km with 90s recovery.
  • HMP runs (Specific): 5–6k blocks at target 6:20/km within a longer run.
🛡️ Injury Prevention
  • With 2 runs/week + 3 strength, your running volume is low-risk — keep it that way.
  • Step-back week every 4th week (−15% volume).
  • Strength focus: glutes, core, single-leg stability — you already do this 3×/week.
  • If legs feel heavy from strength → keep run easy, skip the quality component.
  • Bike rides are great active recovery between runs.
Pre-run and race-day nutrition guidance. You eat high-protein already — these tips focus on fueling around key training sessions and race day. Nothing new on race day — test everything in training first.
🌅 Before Quality Runs (Tuesday)
  • If running in the morning: eat something small 60–90 min before — a banana, toast with honey, or a few rice cakes.
  • If running after work: have a carb-rich snack 1–2 hours before — a banana, an energy bar, or a small bowl of oats.
  • Keep it light and low-fat so your stomach isn’t working hard during intervals/tempo.
  • Have a glass of water beforehand. Coffee is fine if that’s your routine.
📏 Before Long Runs (Saturday)
  • Night before: slightly carb-heavier dinner than usual — extra rice/pasta/potatoes. Keep it familiar.
  • Morning of: eat 60–90 min before. Bigger than Tuesday — e.g. oatmeal + banana, or 2 slices toast with honey + a coffee.
  • Runs > 90 min (W9–10): bring a gel or energy chews. Practice taking one around km 8–10 — this is your race day rehearsal.
🔄 After Runs — Recovery
  • Within the hour: something with carbs + protein. You eat high-protein already — just add carbs. Rice + chicken, yogurt with granola, a protein shake + banana.
  • Rehydrate steadily. Electrolyte tab if it was hot or sweaty.
  • Matters most after long runs and quality sessions.
💧 Hydration
  • Baseline: drink through the day so your pee stays pale.
  • On long runs: sip regularly — a mouthful every 15–20 min.
  • Hot sessions (Jul/Aug): electrolyte tab or sports drink. Munich in October will be cooler.
